About Whiti

Vini Olsen-Reeder (Ngā Pōtiki a Tamapahore), is a budding translator and interpreter. In this world, he see the inefficiencies in the way we currently deal with simultaneous interpretation - it’s too hard to access, and costs too much. The end result is that we forego it in conferences, in the Government sector and in our communities.

We don’t need to forego interpretation, and settling on a better solution has been a goal of his since 2014. Research and value proposition work began soon after. In 2016, Whiti was born as a concept.

Whiti literally means to be in light. Whakawhiti means to bring into light, understanding. Whakawhiti reo means to interpret or translate. The name of the concept carries the ethos of bringing access and understanding to the lives of others, for little cost.

As a business, Whiti.co.nz Limited has been a standalone translation and consultancy business since 2016.

In 2017, Whiti engaged the help of Victoria University Engineering students to help turn the concept of Whiti into something tangible. Their important work is bringing Whiti, and the world of interpretation into life.
